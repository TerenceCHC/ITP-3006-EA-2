Ancient Origins

Football is one of the oldest sports in human history. The earliest recorded form of football dates back over 2,000 years to ancient China, where a game called "Cuju" (蹴鞠) was played during the Warring States period (475–221 BC). The word "Cuju" literally means "kick the ball." During the Han Dynasty, Cuju was used as a military training exercise, with formal rules, dedicated pitches, and even goal keepers. The game reached its peak during the Tang and Song dynasties, becoming a popular activity in the imperial court. In 1958, Dr. João Havelange, then president of FIFA, acknowledged that football originated in China

Meanwhile, in ancient Greece and Rome, a similar ball game called "Harpastum" was played. It involved kicking and carrying a ball across a rectangular field. However, ancient Cuju never developed into the modern game of football with the principle of "fair competition" – that transformation would take place much later in England


The Birth of Modern Football

The modern game of football as we know it today was born in England. A popular legend tells that in the 11th century, after a battle between England and Denmark, the English soldiers kicked the skull of a Danish invader out of anger. Children later joined in, but finding the skull painful to kick, they replaced it with an inflated cow bladder – and thus the modern football was born.

By the 12th century, football matches were being played in English towns, though they were often chaotic and violent, sometimes causing damage to houses and injuries to passers-by.

The first written rules of football, known as the "Cambridge Rules," were established in 1848. These rules introduced the concept of 11 players per side, which came from the fact that each school dormitory housed ten students and one teacher.

The true milestone, however, came on October 26, 1863, when the English Football Association (The FA) was founded in London. On that day, the world's first unified set of football rules was written down, and this date is widely recognised as the birthday of modern football.


Key Milestones

After the formation of The FA, football developed rapidly:

  • 1871 – The FA Cup, the world's oldest football competition, was established.
    • 1872 – The first official international match was played between England and Scotland.
  • 1874 – The offside rule was introduced.
  • 1885 – Professional football was legally recognised in England.
  • 1888 – The English Football League, the world's first national league, was founded.
  • 1896 – Football was included in the first modern Olympic Games.

1904 was another landmark year. On May 21, the Fédération Internationale de Football Association (FIFA) was founded in Paris by seven countries: France, Belgium, Spain, the Netherlands, Denmark, Sweden, and Switzerland. The first FIFA President was Robert Guérin of France.

The greatest event in football – the FIFA World Cup – was the brainchild of Frenchman Jules Rimet, who became FIFA's third president in 1921. In 1928, FIFA voted 25 to 5 to hold a world championship every four years. The first FIFA World Cup was held in Uruguay from July 13 to 30, 1930. Uruguay won the inaugural trophy.


Football Today

Today, football is the world's most popular sport, played in over 200 countries and enjoyed by billions of fans. FIFA now has 211 member associations – more than the United Nations. The FIFA World Cup is the most watched sporting event on the planet, bringing together nations and cultures in a shared celebration of the beautiful game.

From ancient Cuju in China to the global phenomenon it is today, football's journey is a testament to its universal appeal. As the saying goes: "The ball is round, and the game lasts 90 minutes" – but its history spans thousands of years.
